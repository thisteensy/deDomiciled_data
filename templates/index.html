The deDomiciled Project

Welcome to the deDomiciled Project: An exploration of the data of unhoused citizens.

About
The original idea of this project was to explore the changes in the number of unhoused people in each state over time.

The Map
Using available Point in Time counts of unhoused people from the US Department of Housing and Urban Development, I created this map.
As we progress from 2011 to 2019 you can see how some states improve and some states get worse.
You can hover over the states to see the exact numbers of unhoused people per 100,000 residents.
Pay attention to Lousiana. They went from having one of the counts per 100,000 after Hurricaine Katrina to one of the lowest.
This is because in 2011 they implemented the Housing First approach. By 2019 they had cut their number of unhoused people by more than 80%
The Chart
After creating the map I wanted to look at how different factors interacted with houselessness. With this chart you can choose a state and the type of data you want to look at. With the percent change over time feature you can see that in states where availability of low-cost housing was falling faster than poverty houselessness increases.
The Future
In the future I would like to explore other factors that contribute to houselessness, like investment in mental health services.
This data was not available at the current time, but I hope to be able to obtain this for a future release.
It would also be interesting to zoom in on smaller geographic areas, but it was difficult due to inconsistent partitioning of geographic areas across data sources.
Thank you
Thank you for your interest in my project. I hope this visualization helped increase your interest in the crisis of houselessness.
